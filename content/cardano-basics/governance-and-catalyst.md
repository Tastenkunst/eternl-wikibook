# Governance & Project Catalyst

## Catalyst Voting

Cardano is characterized by its commitment to security, scalability, and decentralization. Two key components that reflect this commitment are Catalyst voting and broader blockchain governance. Project Catalyst gives ADA holders the power to propose and vote on ideas that can improve the ecosystem. Catalyst's roots lie between the Shelley and Goguen eras, with the initiative taking shape as part of the roadmap toward the Voltaire phase but technically starting around 2020.

Catalyst kicked off with Fund 0 in September 2020 as a pilot for proposal submission and voting, followed by Fund 1 in October 2020. Each round grows in community participation and funding. Project proposals are reviewed through community feedback and expert opinions, and ADA holders up- and downvote them. Voting power is proportional to ADA holdings, so the community collectively determines which projects receive funding.

Over the years, Project Catalyst has become one of the largest decentralized innovation funds. Using platforms such as IdeaScale, Catalyst continuously refines its processes while funding hundreds of projects in DeFi, governance tooling, and other community initiatives. To date, the rounds have distributed millions of ADA and continue evolving.

## Blockchain governance in Cardano

Blockchain governance covers the mechanisms for deciding protocol rules and future direction. From day one, Cardano aimed to be a decentralized blockchain, gradually transferring decision-making to the community. The Voltaire phase is specifically focused on implementing governance mechanics and a treasury system, moving authority from IOHK to the broad public of ADA holders.

Cardano's development journey is divided into phases: Byron (2017) laid the foundation, Shelley (2020) introduced staking and delegation, Goguen added smart contracts, Basho focused on optimization, and Voltaire now brings governance and sustainability. Governance relies on the treasury system, which collects a portion of fees to fund development and community initiatives. ADA holders can propose protocol changes and vote on them, with voting power tied to holdings. This ensures decisions are made collectively rather than by a single central entity.

Cardano Improvement Proposals (CIPs) formalize how new features or adjustments enter the protocol, inspired by Bitcoin’s BIP process. Project Catalyst also supports governance by funding proposals that extend the blockchain, tightly linking community-driven innovation with protocol evolution.

The integration of Catalyst voting and blockchain governance underscores Cardano’s commitment to decentralization. Catalyst funds community projects, while governance manages protocol amendments and treasury resource allocation. Both rely on ADA-based voting power but focus on different decision layers: Catalyst for project funding and governance for the blockchain’s core rules. Together they keep Cardano dynamic, inclusive, and self-steering.
