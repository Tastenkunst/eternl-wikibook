# Governance & Project Catalyst

Cardano is characterized by its commitment to security, scalability, and decentralization. Two key components that reflect this commitment are **Catalyst voting** and broader **blockchain governance**. While they share the goal of community-driven evolution, they operate at different layers of the ecosystem.

## Catalyst Voting

Project Catalyst gives ADA holders the power to propose and vote on ideas that can improve the ecosystem. Its roots lie between the Shelley and Goguen eras, taking shape as part of the roadmap toward the **Voltaire** phase.

* **Launch**: Kicked off with Fund 0 in September 2020 as a pilot for proposal submission and voting.
* **Process**: Proposals are reviewed through community feedback and expert opinions.
* **Power**: Voting power is strictly proportional to ADA holdings, allowing the community to collectively determine project funding.
* **Scope**: Catalyst funds projects in DeFi, governance tooling, and other community initiatives using platforms like IdeaScale.

## Blockchain Governance in Cardano

Blockchain governance covers the mechanisms for deciding protocol rules and the future direction of the network. The goal is to move authority from IOHK to the broad public of ADA holders.

### The Roadmap to Voltaire

Cardano's development is divided into five distinct phases:
* **Byron (2017)**: Laid the foundation.
* **Shelley (2020)**: Introduced staking and delegation.
* **Goguen**: Added smart contracts.
* **Basho**: Focused on network optimization.
* **Voltaire**: Current phase focusing on governance and sustainability.

### Governance Mechanisms
* **Treasury System**: Collects a portion of fees to fund development and community initiatives.
* **CIPs (Cardano Improvement Proposals)**: Formalized process for new features or protocol adjustments, inspired by Bitcoin’s BIP process.
* **Protocol Decisions**: ADA holders can propose and vote on core protocol changes, ensuring decisions are made collectively rather than by a central entity.

::: info
**Key Distinction**: Catalyst funds **external community projects**, while Governance manages **internal protocol amendments** and treasury resource allocation.
:::
