# Reasons for deleting wallet data and ways to prevent this

If you're facing issues with your wallets disappearing from your Eternl wallet, there could be various reasons. These range from system storage constraints to automated processes that inadvertently affect your wallet data. Here's a breakdown of potential reasons and solutions:

## Full C:/ Disk or Storage
If your devices main drive is full or near full, the operating system can decide it's time to clean up any data it deems unnecessary to make sure the OS can continue running smoothly. The Eternl data is stored locally in the browsers cache, which is a place that is often cleaned up by the OS when the above mechanic triggers.

## Browser Trash Collection
In addition to OS-level cleanup mechanics, modern browsers are designed to optimize performance by clearing cache and other temporary files every once in a while. If Eternl is running as a browser extension or web-app (website), this routine cleanup might clean up wallet data and preferences, making your Eternl unusable.

## 3rd Party Program Setup to Clean Cache
Software designed to clean your system (such as CCleaner) by clearing cache and temporary files might identify wallet data as removable, leading to its removal. 

## Steps to avoid this from happening as much as possible

### Reinstall the Extension
If problems persist, your data was most likely permanently deleted by one of the above mechanics. This would mean you have to reinstall the Eternl wallet extension, as the data it is looking for is no longer on your device.

### Wallet Import/Export
Regularly export your wallet data for safekeeping. If your wallet does disappear, you can import this data back into the wallet once the issue is resolved. This backup can be considered a 'secondary seedphrase', and should be treated as such. It is unwise to upload it to cloud storage, and should be kept offline as much as possible. That said, you should always make sure you have the seedphrase to your wallet, an Eternl backup is a nice and easy way to restore your wallet, but doesn't replace a seedphrase.

> In summary, the disappearance of wallets in your Eternl instance could stem from storage limitations, browser or system cleaning processes, or third-party cleaning applications. Taking steps to ensure persistent data, reinstalling the extension when necessary, and practicing safe data storage can help avoid these issues.
